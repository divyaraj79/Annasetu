# ADR-002 — Database Design

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines the database architecture of AnnaSetu.

It describes every entity currently implemented in the backend, their responsibilities, relationships, and the reasoning behind their existence.

This document represents the official MVP database design and should only be updated if the database schema changes.

---

# Database Philosophy

The database is designed around business entities rather than application screens.

Each table has a single responsibility.

Business logic is intentionally implemented inside the Service Layer rather than the database.

The database stores application state while Services enforce business behavior.

# User

## Purpose

Stores every account in the system.

Authentication information belongs only to User.

Business information belongs to Restaurant or NGO.

## Responsibilities

* Identity
* Name
* Email
* Phone
* Password
* Role
* Account status

## Relationships

User (1:1) Restaurant

User (1:1) NGO

---

# Restaurant

## Purpose

Represents restaurant-specific information.

Restaurant business data is intentionally separated from authentication.

## Responsibilities

* Restaurant profile
* Verification status
* Address
* Geographic location
* Donation ownership

## Relationships

Restaurant (1:N) Donation

---

# NGO

## Purpose

Represents NGO-specific information required for food distribution.

## Responsibilities

* NGO profile
* Verification status
* Address
* Geographic location
* Need ownership
* Match ownership

## Relationships

NGO (1:N) Need

NGO (1:N) Match

---

# Donation

## Purpose

Represents a food donation created by a restaurant.

Donation represents the overall donation event rather than individual food items.

## Responsibilities

* Restaurant ownership
* Pickup location
* Food category
* Vegetarian flag
* Expiry information
* Lifecycle status
* Donation ownership

## Relationships

Donation (N:1) Restaurant

Donation (1:N) DonationItem

Donation (1:N) Match

---

# DonationItem

## Purpose

Represents an individual food item belonging to a Donation.

This normalizes food information while allowing one Donation to contain multiple food items.

## Responsibilities

* Food name
* Quantity
* Quantity unit

## Relationships

DonationItem (N:1) Donation

---

# Need

## Purpose

Represents food requirements published by NGOs.

Needs participate in matching but do not perform matching.

## Responsibilities

* Preferred food category
* Vegetarian preference
* Quantity required
* Quantity unit
* Urgency
* Lifecycle status

## Relationships

Need (N:1) NGO

---

# Match

## Purpose

Represents one matching attempt between a Donation and an NGO.

A Donation may generate multiple Match records.

Final NGO selection is performed by the future MatchingService.

## Responsibilities

* NGO candidate
* Matching score
* Distance
* Match status
* Attempt tracking
* NGO response tracking

## Relationships

Match (N:1) Donation

Match (N:1) NGO

# Primary Design Decisions

## Identity Separation

Authentication data exists only in User.

Restaurant and NGO contain only business-specific information.

---

## Donation Normalization

A Donation represents the overall donation event.

Individual food items are represented using DonationItem.

This separation improves:

* Data normalization
* Analytics
* Dashboard reporting
* AI extraction
* Future scalability

---

## Verification

Restaurants and NGOs maintain independent verification status.

Only approved organizations are allowed to participate in donations or needs.

---

## Matching

Matching is intentionally represented as an independent entity.

This enables:

* Multiple NGO candidates
* Candidate ranking
* Retry attempts
* Future orchestration
* Historical tracking

The final winner selection is intentionally delegated to the future MatchingService.

---

## Soft Delete

All major business entities implement logical deletion using an `is_deleted` flag.

Records are never physically removed from the database during normal business operation.

Soft deletion preserves:

- Historical records
- Relationships
- Auditability
- Referential integrity
- Analytics

Application services exclude deleted entities from normal business operations.

Dependent entities are soft-deleted through Service-layer business cascade rather than database cascade.

Current cascade behavior includes:

- Restaurant → User, Donations, DonationItems, Matches
- NGO → User, Needs, Matches
- Donation → DonationItems, Matches
- Need → Related Matches

This cascade is implemented as business logic to preserve transaction control and future workflow customization.

---

## Registration Rejection Exception

An exception exists for rejected registrations.

Restaurant and NGO registrations that remain in the `PENDING` verification state and are rejected by an administrator are permanently removed together with their associated User account.

This exception is permitted because these registrations never became active business entities and therefore do not participate in:

- Donations
- Needs
- Matches
- Analytics
- Historical business workflows

Once a Restaurant or NGO has been approved, all subsequent deletion follows the standard logical deletion policy.

---

## Geolocation

Users provide addresses.

Latitude and Longitude are generated automatically by the backend through geocoding.

Users never manually provide coordinates.

---

## Database Constraints

AnnaSetu combines database-level constraints with application-level validation.

Database constraints enforce:

- Primary Keys
- Foreign Keys
- Unique Constraints
- Composite Unique Constraints

Application services enforce business-specific rules such as verification, ownership, and lifecycle validation.

Both layers are required to maintain data integrity under concurrent access.
